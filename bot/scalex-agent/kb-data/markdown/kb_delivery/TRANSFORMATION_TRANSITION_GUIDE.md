## Definitions

[DEFINITION]

**Transition**: The process of transferring operational responsibility from 
Client (or incumbent vendor) to new Vendor. Focus is on knowledge transfer 
and operational continuity. Typically 3-6 months.

**Transformation**: Fundamental changes to how services are delivered, including 
new technology, process reengineering, and location changes. Can span 12-24 months.

**Steady State**: Normal operations after transition complete. Performance 
measured against full SLAs.

## Transition Planning (Exhibit 18)

### Transition Phases

**Phase 1: Knowledge Transfer** (Weeks 1-4)
- Document current processes and procedures
- Shadow existing staff performing work
- Inventory all assets, contracts, and licenses
- Identify dependencies and risks
- Establish communication channels

**Phase 2: Parallel Operations** (Weeks 5-8)
- Vendor begins performing services alongside existing team
- Gradual workload transfer by function/application
- Issue identification and resolution
- Process refinement based on learnings
- Go/No-Go checkpoints

**Phase 3: Cutover to Steady State** (Weeks 9-12)
- Full operational responsibility to Vendor
- Hypercare period with elevated support
- Performance stabilization
- Knowledge base finalization
- Transition closeout and lessons learned

## Transition Governance

### Key Roles

**Client Transition Manager**: Single point of accountability on client side
**Vendor Transition Manager**: Vendor counterpart, owns transition plan
**Transition PMO**: Coordinates workstreams, tracks milestones
**Subject Matter Experts**: Provide knowledge transfer for specific areas

| Gate | Criteria |
|------|----------|
| T-30 | Transition plan approved, team staffed |
| T-14 | Knowledge transfer schedule confirmed |
| T-7 | Parallel operations ready |
| Go-Live | All go/no-go criteria met |
| T+30 | Hypercare complete, SLAs active |
| T+90 | Transition formally closed |

## Transition Risk Management

### Common Transition Risks

**Knowledge Loss**
- Risk: Critical knowledge not transferred
- Mitigation: Documented procedures, shadowing, knowledge base

**Staff Retention**
- Risk: Key staff leave during transition
- Mitigation: Retention bonuses, clear communication, career paths

**Integration Failures**
- Risk: Tools/systems don't integrate properly
- Mitigation: Early integration testing, fallback procedures

**Scope Creep**
- Risk: Transition scope expands uncontrolled
- Mitigation: Strict change control, baseline documentation

## Commercial Considerations

### Transition Pricing
- Typically one-time fee or included in rates
- Define what's included vs. chargeable
- Cap transition period duration
- Penalties for delayed transition

### Transformation Pricing
- Separate investment pool or T&M
- Gain-sharing for efficiency improvements
- Innovation credits
- Business case approval process

### At-Risk Provisions
- SLA ramp-up period during transition
- Reduced service credits during stabilization
- Full SLAs activate at steady state
