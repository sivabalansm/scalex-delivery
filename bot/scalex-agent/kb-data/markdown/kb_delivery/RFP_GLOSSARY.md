# Outsourcing Contract Glossary

> Contract definitions and commonly used terms for enterprise outsourcing RFPs and agreements.
>
> Scope: terminology and how it is used in exhibits/SOWs (not vendor benchmarks, rate data, or negotiation playbooks).

---

## A

### Acceptance Testing

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The formal process by which Client verifies that a Deliverable meets agreed requirements and can be accepted (often including test plans, test cases, and acceptance criteria).

**Contract Context**: Appears in SOW deliverables and milestone sections; acceptance often triggers invoicing, starts warranty/support clocks, and determines whether service credits or remedies apply for defects.

**Related Terms**: Deliverable, Go-Live, Service Levels, Steady State

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Affiliate

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: An entity that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a party (control typically defined by ownership/voting power thresholds).

**Contract Context**: Drives scope of who may use services, share confidential information, or receive sublicenses; impacts indemnities, compliance, and whether the Vendor can use affiliates/subcontractors.

**Related Terms**: Agreement, Confidential Information, Intellectual Property

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Agreement

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The contract package governing the relationship (often the Master Services Agreement plus all exhibits, schedules, attachments, SOWs, and amendments incorporated by reference).

**Contract Context**: Used to clarify that exhibits/SOWs are binding and to define order of precedence when documents conflict.

**Related Terms**: Master Services Agreement (MSA), Statement of Work (SOW), Amendment

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Amendment

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A formal written modification to the Agreement executed by authorized representatives of Client and Vendor (often with stated effective date and scope of change).

**Contract Context**: Used for changes that are contractual (not merely operational), such as price changes, scope changes, term extensions, or material legal updates.

**Related Terms**: Change Order, Effective Date, Agreement

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Audit Rights

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Contractual rights allowing Client (or its designee) to examine Vendor's records, processes, controls, and compliance with the Agreement, typically with notice requirements and frequency limits.

**Contract Context**: Usually in MSA body or governance exhibit; defines scope (financial, operational, security, compliance), notice period, frequency caps, cost responsibility, and how findings are remediated.

**Related Terms**: Invoicing, Confidential Information, Benchmarking

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions / MSA

---

## B

### BAFO

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Best and Final Offer; the vendor’s final commercial/technical proposal submitted after negotiation rounds.

**Contract Context**: Used in RFP process and evaluation stages; BAFO terms often become the baseline for final exhibits (pricing, SLAs, governance) and SOW content.

**Related Terms**: RFP, RFQ, Baseline

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Baseline

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The agreed starting point for measurement or change control (e.g., baseline scope, baseline volumes, baseline performance, baseline configuration).

**Contract Context**: Shows up in change control, benchmarking, and SLA measurement sections to prevent disputes over “what changed” and what adjustments apply.

**Related Terms**: Change Order, Benchmarking, Service Levels

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Benchmarking

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A contractual mechanism allowing comparison of pricing and/or service performance to market standards using an agreed benchmark method, and enabling price/service adjustments if materially out of line.

**Contract Context**: Typically in an exhibit (Benchmarking) with rules on frequency, scope, confidentiality, methodology, adjustment thresholds, and dispute process.

**Related Terms**: Contract Year, Pricing, Service Levels

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Business Day

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A day on which banks/companies in the relevant jurisdiction are open for business, excluding weekends and specified holidays (definition varies by location).

**Contract Context**: Used for notice periods, cure periods, invoice dispute timing, and response/approval windows in governance and change control.

**Related Terms**: Effective Date, Termination Assistance, Change Order

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## C

### Change Order

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A written instrument (often in a defined template) that documents a change to scope, pricing, schedule, or service levels, including impact assessment and approvals.

**Contract Context**: Central to controlling scope creep and ensuring changes are priced and scheduled before execution; often paired with order-of-precedence rules and governance approvals.

**Related Terms**: Amendment, Baseline, Statement of Work (SOW)

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Confidential Information

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a party that is designated confidential or reasonably should be understood as confidential (often with exclusions like public domain or independently developed).

**Contract Context**: Governs how Vendor handles Client data, security controls, subcontractor flow-downs, audit rights, and post-termination return/destruction obligations.

**Related Terms**: Intellectual Property, Third-Party Contracts, Transition

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Contract Year

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A defined 12-month period within the contract term (often Year 1 begins on Effective Date or Service Commencement Date; later years follow consecutively).

**Contract Context**: Used for pricing escalators, benchmarking windows, annual service level resets, and year-over-year reporting and governance cadence.

**Related Terms**: Effective Date, Benchmarking, Service Levels

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Cure Period

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The contractually defined time period during which a party may remedy a breach before the other party can exercise termination or other remedies.

**Contract Context**: Appears in termination and default clauses; typically 30-60 days for material breach, shorter for repeated SLA failures or payment defaults.

**Related Terms**: Material Breach, Termination Assistance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions / MSA

---

## D

### Deliverable

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A specified work product or outcome Vendor must provide (e.g., plan, report, configuration, documentation, implemented capability) with acceptance criteria.

**Contract Context**: Listed in SOW/exhibit deliverables tables; acceptance testing and milestone payments are commonly tied to deliverables.

**Related Terms**: Acceptance Testing, Go-Live, Statement of Work (SOW)

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## E

### Effective Date

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The date the Agreement becomes legally effective (often the last signature date, or a specified date in the signature block).

**Contract Context**: Drives contract term, notice periods, when governance obligations start, and can differ from service commencement/go-live dates.

**Related Terms**: Agreement, Contract Year, Business Day

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Excused Performance

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Circumstances where Vendor is not held responsible for failure to meet obligations (e.g., Client-caused delays, approved outages, Force Majeure, or dependencies not met), typically subject to notice and mitigation duties.

**Contract Context**: Common in SLAs and service credits sections; defines when SLA exclusions apply and what evidence/notifications are required.

**Related Terms**: Force Majeure, Service Levels, Service Credits

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## F

### Force Majeure

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Events beyond a party’s reasonable control (e.g., natural disasters, war, certain governmental actions) that delay or prevent performance, usually requiring notice, mitigation, and resumption steps.

**Contract Context**: Impacts SLA exclusions, termination rights after extended events, disaster recovery expectations, and communication obligations.

**Related Terms**: Excused Performance, Disaster Recovery, Business Day

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## G

### Go-Live

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The point at which services or a solution are placed into production use, typically after acceptance testing and cutover activities.

**Contract Context**: Used in transition plans and SOW milestones; go-live can trigger steady-state SLAs and start pricing/invoicing under run-state rates.

**Related Terms**: Transition, Acceptance Testing, Steady State

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Governance

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The operational management structure for the relationship, including meeting cadence, roles, escalation, reporting, decision rights, and change control.

**Contract Context**: Defined in a governance exhibit; referenced by almost every other exhibit to coordinate approvals, issue resolution, and performance management.

**Related Terms**: Service Levels, Change Order, RACI

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## I

### Indemnification

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A contractual obligation for one party to compensate the other for specified losses, damages, or liabilities (e.g., third-party IP claims, data breaches, bodily injury).

**Contract Context**: Usually in MSA body; defines scope of indemnity, exclusions, notice and cooperation requirements, and control of defense. Often subject to liability caps.

**Related Terms**: Liability Cap, Confidential Information, Intellectual Property

**Source**: MSA / Legal Terms

---

### Intellectual Property

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Patents, copyrights, trade secrets, know-how, trademarks, and related rights in inventions/works; often split into pre-existing IP and newly developed IP.

**Contract Context**: Determines ownership/licensing of deliverables, tooling, custom code, documentation, and any transition/exit rights to keep operating after termination.

**Related Terms**: Confidential Information, Statement of Work (SOW), Termination Assistance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## K

### Key Personnel

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Named or role-identified individuals whose skills are critical to delivery, subject to replacement rules, notice, and qualification requirements.

**Contract Context**: Often governed by an exhibit that restricts removal, requires prior approval for replacements, and may impose penalties if turnover exceeds thresholds.

**Related Terms**: Governance, Service Levels, Termination Assistance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## L

### Liability Cap

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A contractual limit on the total damages one party can recover from the other (often expressed as a multiple of annual fees), typically with carve-outs for uncapped items (e.g., IP indemnity, gross negligence, confidentiality breach).

**Contract Context**: Usually in MSA body; defines cap amount, what is included/excluded, and whether caps are per-claim or aggregate.

**Related Terms**: Indemnification, Service Credits

**Source**: MSA / Legal Terms

---

## M

### Master Services Agreement (MSA)

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The master contract setting legal/commercial framework for all services, with SOWs/exhibits adding service-specific requirements.

**Contract Context**: Establishes the baseline legal terms (confidentiality, IP, liability, dispute resolution) and order of precedence for SOWs/exhibits.

**Related Terms**: Agreement, Statement of Work (SOW), Amendment

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Material Breach

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A significant failure to perform contractual obligations that goes to the essence of the Agreement (as opposed to minor or technical breaches).

**Contract Context**: Triggers termination rights after cure period; often defined by examples (failure to provide services, chronic SLA failure, confidentiality breach) rather than precise threshold.

**Related Terms**: Cure Period, Termination Assistance

**Source**: MSA / Legal Terms

---

## R

### RFP

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Request for Proposal; a solicitation requesting vendors propose a solution, scope, service levels, pricing, and delivery approach.

**Contract Context**: RFP materials often become incorporated by reference into the final agreement (with careful control to avoid unintended obligations).

**Related Terms**: RFI, RFQ, BAFO

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### RFI

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Request for Information; a pre-procurement inquiry to gather vendor capabilities and shape the eventual RFP.

**Contract Context**: Used to narrow a long list to a short list and to validate feasibility of scope, tooling, locations, and delivery model assumptions.

**Related Terms**: RFP, RFQ

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### RFQ

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Request for Quotation; a solicitation primarily focused on pricing for defined scope (often used when requirements are stable).

**Contract Context**: RFQs emphasize rate cards, units, and pricing assumptions; SOW and SLA structure still matters, but scope variability is lower.

**Related Terms**: RFP, BAFO, Pricing

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## S

### Service Credits

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Financial remedies (credits) applied when Vendor fails to meet specific service level metrics, typically calculated as a percentage of monthly charges or tower charges with caps.

**Contract Context**: Defined in service level exhibits, including calculation, caps, exclusions, claim mechanics (automatic vs. claimed), and interaction with termination for chronic failure.

**Related Terms**: Service Levels, SLA, Excused Performance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Service Levels

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Performance commitments (metrics + targets) for services (availability, response time, resolution time, quality, timeliness), including measurement rules.

**Contract Context**: Implemented via an SLA exhibit and referenced by tower SOWs; defines reporting, measurement, exclusions, remedies, and chronic failure rules.

**Related Terms**: SLA, Service Credits, Governance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### SLA

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Service Level Agreement; the structured set of service level metrics, targets, measurement methodology, reporting, and remedies.

**Contract Context**: Typically an exhibit with per-tower metrics and service credit mechanisms; may include ramp-up periods (transition vs steady state).

**Related Terms**: Service Levels, Service Credits, Steady State

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Statement of Work (SOW)

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The service-specific document defining scope, deliverables, roles/responsibilities, service levels, governance touchpoints, assumptions, and dependencies for a service tower or project.

**Contract Context**: SOWs are the operational backbone of the Agreement; they define what Vendor must do and are the primary reference for disputes about scope.

**Related Terms**: Deliverable, Change Order, MSA

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Steady State

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The normal operational phase after transition completes and services are stabilized; full SLAs and standard operating processes apply.

**Contract Context**: Used to separate transition obligations (plans, knowledge transfer) from run operations; SLAs may be reduced or staged during transition/hypercare.

**Related Terms**: Transition, Go-Live, Service Levels

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Subcontractor

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A third party engaged by Vendor to perform part of the Services, typically subject to Client approval, flow-down of contract terms, and Vendor remaining responsible for performance.

**Contract Context**: Usually in MSA or governance exhibit; defines approval requirements (pre-approved list vs. case-by-case), flow-down obligations, and Vendor liability for subcontractor performance.

**Related Terms**: Affiliate, Key Personnel, Third-Party Contracts

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions / MSA

---

## T

### Termination Assistance

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Obligations requiring Vendor to support an orderly exit/transition-out on termination or expiry, including knowledge transfer, documentation, data return, and cooperation with replacement vendors.

**Contract Context**: Usually an exhibit detailing scope, duration, resources, rates, and deliverables; critical to reduce operational disruption and lock-in risk.

**Related Terms**: Transition, Intellectual Property, Confidential Information

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Transition

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The phase and activities required to move services from Client or incumbent vendor to the selected Vendor (knowledge transfer, cutover, stabilization).

**Contract Context**: Defined in a transition exhibit and attachments (plans, risk management) and referenced by each SOW’s onboarding requirements.

**Related Terms**: Go-Live, Steady State, Termination Assistance

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

### Transformation

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Planned improvements or changes to operating model, tooling, processes, automation, or architecture beyond “lift-and-shift” transition (often managed as a program of initiatives).

**Contract Context**: Usually an exhibit describing governance, roadmaps, investment approval, deliverables, and how transformation interacts with pricing and service levels.

**Related Terms**: Transition, Governance, Deliverable

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions

---

## W

### Warranty

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: A contractual assurance that services or deliverables will meet specified standards (e.g., defect-free, fit for purpose, compliant with specs) for a defined period, with remedies for breach.

**Contract Context**: Appears in SOWs for deliverables and MSA for services; defines warranty period, remedy (re-perform, fix, credit), and exclusions (Client-caused issues, unauthorized changes).

**Related Terms**: Acceptance Testing, Deliverable, Service Levels

**Source**: Exhibit 1 - Definitions / MSA

---

### Work Authorization

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The formal approval mechanism for authorizing specific work (projects, enhancements, out-of-scope requests) including scope, schedule, and cost before work begins.

**Contract Context**: Appears in governance and change control sections; defines who can authorize, approval thresholds, required documentation, and relationship to pricing/invoicing.

**Related Terms**: Change Order, Statement of Work (SOW), Governance

**Source**: Exhibit 5 - Governance / Exhibit 1 - Definitions

## Additional Template-Extracted Definitions (Selected)

> The entries below are pulled from specific template documents in `RFP Template Package 2019-07-03/` (lightly cleaned to use Client/Vendor and remove boilerplate). They are included because they are commonly referenced in pricing units, service management, or SOW scoping.

### Access Management

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The process for allowing End Users to make use of the Services, data, or other assets.

**Contract Context**: Often appears in security/IAM scope language to define who provisions access, how access requests are approved, and how access is audited.

**Related Terms**: Confidential Information, Intellectual Property, Service Levels

**Source**: Security Services SOW template (file labeled “Exhibit 2.8 Security Services”)

---

### Inflation Sensitivity Index

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: The portion or percentage of Vendor charges that are inflation sensitive, as set forth in the pricing forms.

**Contract Context**: Used to apply indexation/escalation only to the cost components intended to float with inflation (and not to pass-throughs or non-inflationary components).

**Related Terms**: Pricing, Contract Year, Benchmarking

**Source**: Pricing and Financial Provisions template (file labeled “Exhibit 4 Pricing and Financial Provisions”)

---

### Overhead Functions

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Non-chargeable functions that are provided by Vendor as part of Vendor’s overall project management and contract management.

**Contract Context**: Used to prevent “double charging” for management overhead on top of run/service fees; typically tied to staffing and invoicing rules.

**Related Terms**: Pricing, Invoicing, Governance

**Source**: Pricing and Financial Provisions template (file labeled “Exhibit 4 Pricing and Financial Provisions”)

---

### Tier 1 — Mission Critical (Storage)

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Storage that is used for mission-critical applications with broad visibility and impact.

**Contract Context**: Used in resource-unit definitions, service parameters, and pricing to tier storage services and align requirements, SLAs, and costs to criticality.

**Related Terms**: Service Levels, Disaster Recovery, Deliverable

**Source**: Resource Unit Definitions template (Attachment 4-E)

---

### Tier 2 — Business Critical (Storage)

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Storage that is used for business-critical applications and vital systems with broad visibility and impact.

**Contract Context**: Used in resource-unit definitions and pricing tiers; often paired with different RTO/RPO and backup/restore expectations.

**Related Terms**: Disaster Recovery, Service Levels

**Source**: Resource Unit Definitions template (Attachment 4-E)

---

### Tier 3 — Mass Storage

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Online mass storage that is used for short-term retention requirements.

**Contract Context**: Used for lower-cost tiers with different performance and availability characteristics; should align to data retention and backup policies.

**Related Terms**: Data Center Services, Backup, Service Levels

**Source**: Resource Unit Definitions template (Attachment 4-E)

---

### Tier 4 — Archive

[DEFINITION]

**Definition**: Online mass storage that is used for long-term retention requirements.

**Contract Context**: Used for archival storage tiers; requirements often center on retention, retrieval SLAs, and legal/compliance hold needs rather than performance.

**Related Terms**: Data Center Services, Confidential Information

**Source**: Resource Unit Definitions template (Attachment 4-E)
