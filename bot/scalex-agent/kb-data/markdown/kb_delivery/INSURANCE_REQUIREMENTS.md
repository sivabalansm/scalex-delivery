## Cyber Liability Insurance

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Why It Matters
Cyber liability insurance covers costs associated with data breaches and cyber incidents, including notification costs, forensic investigation, legal defense, regulatory fines, and business interruption.

### Coverage Components

**First-Party Coverage:**
- Breach response costs (notification, credit monitoring)
- Forensic investigation
- Data restoration
- Business interruption / system failure
- Cyber extortion / ransomware
- Reputational harm

**Third-Party Coverage:**
- Privacy liability (regulatory actions, lawsuits)
- Network security liability
- Media liability
- Payment card industry (PCI) fines

### Minimum Limits by Data Volume

| Data Records | Recommended Minimum | Notes |
|--------------|---------------------|-------|
| < 100,000 | $2-5M | Small engagement |
| 100,000 - 1M | $5-10M | Standard enterprise |
| 1M - 10M | $10-15M | Large enterprise |
| > 10M | $15-25M+ | Major data handler |

### Key Policy Terms to Verify

[CHECKLIST] Cyber Policy Review
- [ ] Coverage includes vendor/third-party access
- [ ] Retroactive date covers contract start
- [ ] No exclusion for acts of employees/contractors
- [ ] Regulatory defense and fines covered
- [ ] PCI fines and assessments covered (if applicable)
- [ ] Business interruption included
- [ ] Social engineering / fraudulent transfer covered
- [ ] Cloud service provider incidents covered

## Professional Liability / Errors & Omissions (E&O)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Why It Matters
Professional liability covers claims arising from negligent acts, errors, or omissions in professional service delivery. Essential for technology and business process outsourcing.

### Coverage Scope
- Negligent acts or omissions in service delivery
- Failure to deliver contracted services
- Breach of professional duty
- Intellectual property infringement (technology services)
- Unintentional breach of confidentiality

### Minimum Limits by Contract Value

| Annual Contract Value | Recommended Minimum |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| < $1M | $1-2M |
| $1M - $5M | $5M |
| $5M - $20M | $10M |
| > $20M | $15-25M |

### Key Policy Terms

[CHECKLIST] E&O Policy Review
- [ ] Services provided match policy description
- [ ] No exclusion for contracted services
- [ ] Defense costs inside or outside limits (outside preferred)
- [ ] Covers subcontractor acts
- [ ] Extended reporting period (tail coverage) available
- [ ] Retroactive date appropriate

**Source**: Professional services industry standards

## General Liability (CGL)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Why It Matters
Commercial General Liability covers third-party claims for bodily injury, property damage, and personal/advertising injury. Standard business protection.

### Standard Limits

| Limit Type | Standard | Premium |
|------------|----------|---------|
| Per Occurrence | $1,000,000 | $2,000,000 |
| General Aggregate | $2,000,000 | $5,000,000 |
| Products/Completed Ops | $2,000,000 | $5,000,000 |
| Personal/Advertising Injury | $1,000,000 | $2,000,000 |

### Additional Insured Requirements

**Client should be named as Additional Insured for:**
- Ongoing operations
- Completed operations
- Products liability (if applicable)

**Additional Insured Endorsement Language:**
"[Client Name] is added as an Additional Insured, but only with respect to liability arising out of [Vendor]'s operations performed on behalf of, or services provided to, the Additional Insured."

**Source**: CGL standard policy forms

## Workers Compensation & Employer's Liability

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Requirements

**Workers Compensation:**
- Statutory limits as required by applicable state/jurisdiction
- Coverage in all states where work is performed
- USL&H (Longshore) coverage if applicable
- Foreign coverage if international operations

**Employer's Liability:**
- $1,000,000 per accident (standard)
- $1,000,000 per employee for disease
- $1,000,000 policy limit for disease

### Verification Requirements

[CHECKLIST] Workers Comp Review
- [ ] Coverage in all applicable jurisdictions
- [ ] No exclusions for client locations
- [ ] Alternate employer endorsement (if rebadging)
- [ ] Experience modification rate (EMR) reviewed

**Note**: EMR > 1.0 indicates worse than average safety record; may warrant additional inquiry.

**Source**: State workers compensation requirements

## Crime / Fidelity Insurance

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Why It Matters
Covers losses from employee dishonesty, theft, fraud, and forgery. Critical when vendor employees have access to client funds, data, or assets.

### Coverage Components
- Employee dishonesty / theft
- Forgery or alteration
- Computer fraud
- Funds transfer fraud
- Money and securities
- Social engineering fraud

### Minimum Limits

| Access Type | Recommended Minimum |
|-------------|---------------------|
| No financial access | $500K |
| Limited financial access | $1-2M |
| Significant financial access | $5M+ |
| Treasury/payment processing | $10M+ |

### Key Policy Terms

[CHECKLIST] Crime Policy Review
- [ ] Third-party coverage (client as loss payee)
- [ ] Computer fraud coverage included
- [ ] Social engineering coverage included
- [ ] Coverage extends to subcontractor employees
- [ ] No exclusion for acts discovered after termination

**Source**: Crime insurance standards

## Certificate of Insurance Requirements

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Certificate Requirements

**Standard COI Must Include:**
- Named insured (vendor legal entity)
- Policy numbers for each coverage
- Policy effective and expiration dates
- Coverage limits for each policy
- Certificate holder (client) information
- Additional insured status (where required)
- Waiver of subrogation (where required)

### Contract Language

**Certificate Delivery:**
"Vendor shall provide certificates of insurance evidencing the required coverages:
(a) Within 10 business days of contract execution
(b) At least 30 days prior to any policy renewal
(c) Within 10 business days upon Client request"

**Notice of Cancellation:**
"Vendor shall provide Client with 30 days' prior written notice of cancellation, non-renewal, or material change to any required insurance policy."

**Maintenance Obligation:**
"Vendor shall maintain the required insurance coverages throughout the term of this Agreement and for a period of [2-3 years] following termination for any claims arising from services performed."

### Certificate Review Checklist

[CHECKLIST] COI Review
- [ ] All required coverages listed
- [ ] Limits meet or exceed minimums
- [ ] Policies are current (not expired)
- [ ] Certificate holder is correct
- [ ] Additional insured status confirmed (if required)
- [ ] Waiver of subrogation noted (if required)
- [ ] No exclusions that would void coverage for our work

## Contract Exhibit Language Template

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

### Sample Insurance Exhibit

**EXHIBIT [X] - INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS**

**1. Required Coverage**

Vendor shall maintain, at its sole cost and expense, the following insurance coverages:

(a) **Commercial General Liability**: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate
(b) **Professional Liability / E&O**: $[5,000,000] per claim / aggregate
(c) **Cyber Liability**: $[10,000,000] per claim / aggregate
(d) **Workers Compensation**: Statutory limits
(e) **Employer's Liability**: $1,000,000 per accident
(f) **Crime / Fidelity**: $[2,000,000]

**2. Policy Requirements**

All policies shall:
(a) Be issued by insurers rated A- VIII or better by A.M. Best
(b) Be primary and non-contributory
(c) Include waiver of subrogation in favor of Client
(d) Name Client as Additional Insured (CGL only)

**3. Evidence of Insurance**

Vendor shall provide certificates of insurance within 10 business days of execution and annually thereafter. Vendor shall provide 30 days' notice of cancellation or material change.

**4. Claims-Made Policies**

For claims-made policies (E&O, Cyber), Vendor shall maintain coverage for [3] years following termination or procure an extended reporting period.
