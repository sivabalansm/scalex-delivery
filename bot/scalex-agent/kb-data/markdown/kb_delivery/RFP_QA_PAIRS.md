## RFP_QA_001: What documents should be included in an outsourcing RFP?

A comprehensive outsourcing RFP package typically includes: (1) RFP Instructions (response rules, timeline, evaluation criteria), (2) Exhibit 1 - Definitions, (3) Exhibit 2.x - SOWs for each in-scope service tower, (4) Exhibit 3 - Pricing requirements and forms, (5) Exhibit 4 - Service Levels and service credits, (6) Exhibit 5 - Governance, and (7) Appendices documenting current state. Add additional exhibits as needed (HR/rebadging, third-party contracts, key personnel, DR, invoicing/payment, benchmarking, termination assistance).

## RFP_QA_002: What is a Statement of Work (SOW) in outsourcing?

A Statement of Work (SOW) is the service-specific document that defines what the Vendor must deliver. It should include: scope boundaries (in/out), service descriptions, roles and responsibilities (RACI), deliverables and acceptance criteria, tower-specific service levels, tooling requirements, assumptions and dependencies, reporting requirements, and transition/hypercare requirements. In multi-tower deals, separate SOWs are typically used for each tower (e.g., Data Center, Network, EUC).

**Tags:** sow, scope, structure, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 2.x - Statements of Work

## RFP_QA_003: What is a service credit in an SLA?

A service credit is a contractual financial remedy applied when the Vendor misses an SLA target. A complete service credit structure defines: the metric-to-credit mapping, calculation formula, when credits apply, exclusions (e.g., approved outages/excused performance), caps (per metric/tower/month), and how credits are applied to invoices. It should also define chronic failure escalation (e.g., repeated misses trigger corrective action plans and potentially termination rights).

**Tags:** sla, service-credits, remedies, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 4 - Service Levels

## RFP_QA_004: What pricing models are used in outsourcing contracts?

Common outsourcing pricing models include fixed price (for stable, well-defined scope), unit-based pricing (per transaction/output with clear unit definitions and volume rules), time and materials (T&M) for variable scope, and outcome-based pricing for defined business outcomes. Most contracts use a hybrid: run services are often unit-based or fixed, while projects/transformation work may be T&M with governance and change control.

**Tags:** pricing, exhibit-3, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 3 - Pricing

## RFP_QA_005: What is governance in an outsourcing relationship?

Governance is the operating framework for managing the Client/Vendor relationship. A complete governance exhibit defines meeting forums (operational/tactical/strategic), cadence, attendees, escalation paths, decision rights, required reports (KPIs, SLAs, risk log, change log), and how issues/risks/changes are managed. Good governance prevents ambiguity about who decides and how problems are resolved.

**Tags:** governance, exhibit-5, structure, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 5 - Governance

## RFP_QA_006: What is the difference between transition and transformation in outsourcing?

Transition is the controlled handover of services from Client (or incumbent) to the Vendor: knowledge transfer, cutover, and stabilization. Transformation is the set of planned improvements beyond lift-and-shift (automation, tooling changes, process redesign, platform modernization). Contracts typically define transition as a time-bound phase with plans and gates, and transformation as a governed program of initiatives with deliverables and approvals.

**Tags:** transition, transformation, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 16 - Transition / Exhibit 18 - Transformation

## RFP_QA_007: What current state documentation do I need for an RFP?

Current state documentation should give vendors enough detail to price, plan transition, and operate services. At minimum: inventories (apps, servers, endpoints, network devices), topology/architecture diagrams where relevant, process descriptions (incident/change), volumes (tickets/transactions), tooling landscape, constraints (security/compliance), and dependencies on Client or third parties. Appendices are typically organized by tower (data center, network, EUC, applications, cross-functional, cloud, HR).

**Tags:** current-state, appendices, how-to  
**Source:** Appendices A-I

## RFP_QA_008: What should be included in a termination assistance exhibit?

A termination assistance exhibit should define: triggers (expiry/termination), assistance scope (knowledge transfer, documentation, data export, parallel run, training), deliverables (runbooks, inventories, transition plan), duration and staffing expectations, rates and caps (included vs billable), cooperation obligations with replacement vendors, and data return/credential handoff procedures. The goal is an orderly transition-out without service interruption.

**Tags:** termination-assistance, exit, exhibit-17, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 17 - Termination Assistance

## RFP_QA_009: What should I include in a pricing exhibit?

Include: pricing model(s) by tower/service, a complete rate card (roles/units/assumptions/inclusions), baseline volumes and volume-band rules, indexation/escalators and timing, pass-through cost rules (evidence + markups), invoice structure requirements, true-ups/adjustments, and change order pricing method. If transition/transformation fees exist, define what is included, when billed, and how scope changes are handled.

## RFP_QA_010: How do I structure service levels for service desk?

Define: priority/severity model (with examples), response and resolution targets by priority, abandonment/ASA targets for phone/chat (if applicable), first contact resolution definition and target, backlog thresholds, major incident communications timelines, and reporting cadence. Add measurement rules (clock start/stop), exclusions (approved outages/excused performance), and service credit mechanics (credits, caps, chronic failure).

**Tags:** service-desk, sla, exhibit-4, how-to

## RFP_QA_011: What is benchmarking in an outsourcing contract?

Benchmarking is a contractual right to compare pricing and/or service performance to market standards under defined rules. A complete benchmarking exhibit defines when it can be done (frequency and timing), what scope is included, how the benchmark is conducted (benchmarker selection, confidentiality), and what happens if results are materially out of line (adjustment thresholds, phased changes, dispute process).

**Tags:** benchmarking, exhibit-12, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 12 - Benchmarking

## RFP_QA_012: What are common SLA metrics for service desk?

Common metrics include: response time and resolution time by priority, abandonment rate and average speed of answer (for calls), first contact resolution rate (with a clear definition), ticket backlog thresholds, reopen rate, customer satisfaction (CSAT) sampling method, and major incident communication timeliness. Each metric should include measurement rules, reporting cadence, and exclusions.

**Tags:** service-desk, sla, metrics, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 4 - Service Levels / Exhibit 2.1 - Cross-Functional Services

## RFP_QA_013: How do I protect against vendor lock-in in an outsourcing contract?

Use structural contract mechanisms: a strong termination assistance exhibit (scope, deliverables, duration, rates/caps), explicit data return/export requirements (format and timing), clear IP and documentation rights to continue operations post-exit, obligations to cooperate with replacement vendors, and controls over third-party contracts and tooling dependencies (assignment/consent rules). Also define ownership of runbooks, configurations, and service records produced during delivery.

**Tags:** exit, termination-assistance, ip, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 17 - Termination Assistance / Exhibit 7 - Third-Party Contracts / Exhibit 1 - Definitions

## RFP_QA_014: What is a RACI matrix in outsourcing?

RACI is a responsibility assignment model: Responsible (does the work), Accountable (owns the outcome), Consulted (provides input), and Informed (kept updated). In outsourcing, RACI should be included per tower and per key process (incident, change, request, security response) and must include third parties/resolver groups so ownership is unambiguous during escalations.

**Tags:** raci, roles, sow, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 2.x - Statements of Work / Exhibit 5 - Governance

## RFP_QA_015: How long should a transition period be?

Transition length depends on scope complexity, number of towers, and the quality of current state documentation. Contracts commonly structure transition as a gated plan (knowledge transfer → parallel run → cutover → hypercare) rather than a single duration. Your transition exhibit and attachments should define phases, milestones, entry/exit criteria, and the point at which steady-state SLAs apply.

**Tags:** transition, planning, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 16 - Transition

## RFP_QA_016: What is a service credit pool?

A service credit pool is a contractual cap or bucket limiting the total credits that can be applied within a period (e.g., per month or per quarter), sometimes by tower. It should define the pool amount, how credits are prioritized if multiple metrics miss, whether unused credits roll over, and how the pool interacts with chronic failure remedies and termination rights.

**Tags:** service-credits, sla, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 4 - Service Levels

---

## RFP_QA_017: What is excused performance in an SLA?

Excused performance defines conditions where the Vendor is not held responsible for missing a service level (e.g., Client-caused delays, approved maintenance, Force Majeure) if notice and mitigation requirements are met. A complete SLA exhibit specifies what is excused, what evidence is required, how the SLA clock is paused/stopped, and how disputes are resolved.

**Tags:** sla, exclusions, excused-performance, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 4 - Service Levels / Exhibit 1 - Definitions

## RFP_QA_018: How do I structure governance meetings in an outsourcing contract?

Define a tiered cadence: operational meetings (weekly) for day-to-day delivery, tactical reviews (monthly) for performance and planning, and strategic steering (quarterly) for roadmap and escalations. For each, specify attendees, required inputs (dashboards, risk log, change log), decision rights, and escalation paths. The governance exhibit should also define reporting standards and how decisions are documented.

**Tags:** governance, meetings, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 5 - Governance

## RFP_QA_019: How do I define a change order process?

Define the steps and artifacts: request submission, impact assessment (scope, schedule, cost, SLAs), options and assumptions, approvals (who can approve and at what thresholds), implementation plan, and documentation updates (SOW, pricing, SLAs). The process should reference baseline scope/volumes and specify when work may begin (typically after approval).

**Tags:** change-control, change-order, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 1 - Definitions / Exhibit 5 - Governance

## RFP_QA_020: What should be included in a key personnel exhibit?

Include: list of key roles (and/or named individuals), minimum qualifications, coverage expectations (time zones/on-call), replacement rules (notice, approval, overlap/transition), turnover limits, and remedies for noncompliance. The exhibit should also clarify whether key personnel rules apply to subcontractors or affiliates.

**Tags:** key-personnel, exhibit-8, how-to

## RFP_QA_021: What should be included in an invoicing exhibit?

Include: invoice structure requirements (tower breakdown, units, volumes, rates), timing and payment terms, supporting documentation requirements, dispute timelines (how long Client has to dispute and what is paid while disputed), audit rights for invoice validation, credit application mechanics (service credits/true-ups), and record retention requirements.

**Tags:** invoicing, payments, exhibit-11, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 11 - Invoicing and Payment

## RFP_QA_022: How do I structure DR requirements in an outsourcing contract?

Use a tiering model (criticality tiers) and define RTO/RPO per tier. Specify DR architecture expectations, roles during a disaster, communications plan, testing frequency and success criteria, and required documentation deliverables. Clarify dependencies and exclusions (what Client provides, what is out of scope) and how remediation from failed tests is handled.

**Tags:** disaster-recovery, rto, rpo, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 10 - Disaster Recovery

---

## RFP_QA_023: How do I define scope boundaries in a tower SOW?

Define in-scope services, explicit out-of-scope items, and demarcations with other towers and third parties. Include an inventory baseline where possible (apps, devices, sites), and document assumptions and dependencies (Client approvals, access, facilities). Tie scope changes to the change order process and baseline definitions so changes are controlled and priced.

**Tags:** scope, demarcation, sow, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 2.x - Statements of Work / Exhibit 5 - Governance

---

## RFP_QA_024: What should a cloud services SOW include?

Include: cloud provider/account scope, operating model and on-call coverage, provisioning controls and guardrails, monitoring/logging requirements, backup/DR responsibilities, security/IAM responsibilities, cost management responsibilities (tagging and reporting), and clear separation of consumption costs (pass-through) from managed services fees.

**Tags:** cloud, sow, exhibit-2.9, how-to

## RFP_QA_025: What should a SIAM SOW include?

Include: SIAM authority and decision rights, integrated incident/problem/change model across vendors, standardized KPI definitions and dashboards, tool integration requirements (ITSM/CMDB/monitoring), escalation and major incident coordination procedures, service catalog ownership, and continual service improvement deliverables and cadence.

**Tags:** siam, integration, sow, exhibit-2.10, how-to  
**Source:** Exhibit 2.10 - Service Integration and Management (SIAM)

## RFP_QA_026: What should be included in a third-party contracts exhibit?

Include: a list of in-scope third-party contracts/licenses, the desired disposition (assign/novate/replace/terminate), consent requirements and responsibilities, cost responsibility (fees/penalties/renewals), continuity plan during transition (parallel run/cutover timing), and reporting/recordkeeping requirements for renewal dates and contract copies.
