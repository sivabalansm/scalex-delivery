# Contract Provisions Guide (Outsourcing Exhibits)

> Practical structure and completeness guidance for common outsourcing contract exhibits. Focus: “how to structure the document” and “what must be present”.

**Note on exhibit numbering**: Exhibit numbers are not universal across template packages. Use the **topic/title** as the primary reference, and treat exhibit numbers as package-specific labels.

---

## Pricing (Exhibit 3)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Define how the Vendor will be paid, what is included, how pricing changes over time, and how charges are validated and disputed.

**Key Provisions**:
- Pricing model(s) (fixed, unit-based, T&M, outcome-based) and which services use each model
- Rate card definitions (roles, units, assumptions, minimums, and inclusions)
- Volume/consumption rules (baseline volumes, bands, true-ups, and how volume changes are handled)
- Indexation/escalators and when they apply (contract year timing)
- Pass-through costs and markups (if any) plus required evidence
- Out-of-scope and change order pricing method
- Transition and transformation fees (one-time vs embedded) and what is included

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Whether service credits can offset invoices automatically and whether there is a monthly cap
- How tightly “included” services are defined (to prevent nickle-and-dime change orders)
- Audit rights and the level of invoice detail required to verify charges

[CHECKLIST] Exhibit 3 Review
- [ ] Pricing model clearly mapped to each tower/service
- [ ] Definitions of units/transactions are unambiguous and measurable
- [ ] Baseline volumes and adjustment mechanism documented
- [ ] Rate card includes inclusions/exclusions and assumptions
- [ ] Indexation/escalators defined (timing, index, cap/floor)
- [ ] Pass-through cost rules and evidence requirements included
- [ ] Change order pricing method included

**Source**: Exhibit 3 - Pricing

## Service Levels (Exhibit 4)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Define measurable service performance commitments and the remedies when commitments are not met.

**Key Provisions**:
- Metric definitions (what is measured and how the clock starts/stops)
- Targets and measurement frequency
- Data sources and calculation rules (including dispute process)
- Exclusions (planned maintenance, approved outages, excused performance)
- Service credit mechanics (calculation, caps, application timing)
- Chronic failure definition and escalation/remedy path (including termination rights)

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Whether credits are automatic vs. claimed by Client
- Level of caps (per metric, per tower, overall monthly cap) and whether credits stack
- What counts as excused performance and the required proof/notice

[CHECKLIST] Exhibit 4 Review
- [ ] Each critical service has metrics and targets
- [ ] Measurement methodology is auditable (data sources + formulas)
- [ ] Exclusions are explicitly listed and limited
- [ ] Service credit calculation and caps are defined
- [ ] Chronic failure and escalation path are defined
- [ ] Reporting cadence and format are specified

**Source**: Exhibit 4 - Service Levels

## Governance (Exhibit 5)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Key Provisions**:
- Governance bodies (operational/tactical/strategic) with cadence and attendees

- Issue/risk management processes and required artifacts

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Who has final decision rights for scope/standards/tools
- What meetings/reports are mandatory versus optional
- Whether Vendor must provide tooling/dashboards or just data feeds

- [ ] Decision rights matrix included

## Third-Party Contracts (Exhibit 7)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Key Provisions**:

- Consent requirements and who obtains consents
- Responsibility for fees, penalties, and ongoing obligations
- Service continuity during transition (parallel runs, cutover timing)
- Records and evidence requirements (contract copies, renewal dates)

- Who bears risk if consents cannot be obtained
- Whether Vendor must assume liability for inherited contracts
- Timing and control of contract renewals during transition

## Key Personnel (Exhibit 8)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Reduce delivery risk by ensuring critical roles are staffed by qualified individuals and cannot be replaced casually.

**Key Provisions**:
- List of key roles/named individuals and minimum qualification criteria
- Replacement rules (notice period, approval requirement, overlap/transition)
- Limits on turnover and remedies for excessive churn
- Role coverage expectations (on-call, availability, location/shift requirements)

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Whether Client approval is required for replacements (and what “approval” means)
- Overlap duration and whether replacement costs are billable
- Whether key personnel obligations apply to subcontractors/affiliates

[CHECKLIST] Exhibit 8 Review
- [ ] Key roles clearly listed with qualifications
- [ ] Replacement notice and approval process defined
- [ ] Transition/overlap requirements defined
- [ ] Turnover limits and remedies defined
- [ ] Coverage expectations defined (hours, time zones, on-call)

**Source**: Exhibit 8 - Key Personnel

## Benchmarking (Exhibit 12)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Allow Client to compare services and/or pricing to market and adjust if materially out of line, under controlled rules.

**Key Provisions**:
- Frequency and timing of benchmark (e.g., after steady state and then every X years)
- Scope (which towers/services; pricing vs performance; excluded new services)
- Benchmarker selection and confidentiality obligations
- Adjustment mechanism (thresholds, phased implementation, caps, dispute path)
- Data access and cooperation requirements

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Threshold for “out of line” and size/timing of adjustments
- Who pays for the benchmark and under what conditions
- Limits on scope to avoid constant repricing

[CHECKLIST] Exhibit 12 Review
- [ ] Benchmark timing and frequency defined
- [ ] Scope defined (pricing/performance; included towers)
- [ ] Benchmarker selection and confidentiality defined
- [ ] Adjustment thresholds and mechanics defined
- [ ] Cooperation/data access requirements defined

**Source**: Exhibit 12 - Benchmarking

## Termination Assistance (Exhibit 17)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Ensure orderly exit/transition-out so Client can switch vendors or insource without service interruption.

**Key Provisions**:
- Triggers (expiry, termination for convenience/cause) and required notice
- Assistance scope (knowledge transfer, documentation, data export, parallel run)
- Deliverables (runbooks, asset lists, credentials handoff process, training)
- Duration and rates (included vs billable; capped hours; rate card)
- Cooperation obligations with replacement vendors and third parties

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Whether termination assistance is included in baseline fees or separately billed
- Duration and required staffing levels
- Rights to tools, scripts, and documentation needed to operate after exit

- [ ] Duration and staffing expectations defined
- [ ] Pricing method and caps defined
- [ ] Data return/export obligations defined (format + timing)
- [ ] Cooperation obligations with replacement vendors defined

- Format and delivery method (portal, email, dashboard, file format)
- Data sources and accuracy requirements
- Report review and approval process
- Ad-hoc and custom report request process (included vs. billable)

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Whether dashboards/portals are included or separately priced
- Data retention and historical report access
- Who owns report templates and underlying data

- How verbatim feedback is shared and acted upon

## Service Tower Demarcation (Exhibit 15)

[CONTRACT PROVISION]

**Purpose**: Define scope boundaries and handoff points between service towers and/or vendors to prevent gaps and overlaps.

**Key Provisions**:
- Tower-by-tower scope summary (what each tower includes/excludes)
- Handoff points and trigger events (when responsibility transfers)
- Escalation and coordination model for cross-tower issues
- RACI for shared processes (e.g., incident escalation, change coordination)
- Tool integration requirements for cross-tower visibility
- Dispute resolution for scope boundary disagreements

**Common Negotiation Points**:
- Who owns "gray area" issues that span towers
- How multi-vendor coordination is governed
- Whether a SIAM function is required for integration
